This lesson continues the discussion of J2EE and the need for standardization in multi-tier systems.  It presents J2EE as the answer to the need for standardization. 

It provides a simple example of the need for transaction processing involving your saving account and your checking account, and explains that enterprise applications require a variety of standard services, of which transaction processing is only one. 

The lesson discusses the shift of responsibility for programming standard services from the application programmer to the experts. 

The standardization of multi-tier systems provided by Sun's J2EE extends the benefits of Write Once, Run Anywhere to multi-tier enterprise applications, making it possible for application programmers to develop software for a variety of brands and models of multi-tier servers without having to learn the peculiarities of each.
